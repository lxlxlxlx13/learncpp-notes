Advanced Taxation Revision

Question

A is a publisher of educational books. It is planning to use Singapore as the ASEAN base for the distribution of books published in the UK.

Under this plan, A will arrange to have books imported into Singapore for distribution to distributors in the various ASEAN countries. The contracts for the sale of books will be concluded directly by A with the various ASEAN country distributors and these distributors will be invoiced directly by A.

The revenue from these ASEAN distributors will be booked as A’s revenue and it is estimated that annual sales to the ASEAN region will be in the region of S$8m to S$10m. All the logistics will be undertaken by an unrelated logistics company operating in Singapore.

For the coordination of its ASEAN distribution activity, A will incorporate a wholly owned subsidiary in Singapore (SS). SS will be responsible for the liaison and co-ordination of the importation and delivery of books to the distributors in the various ASEAN countries and will receive a service fee from A for this liaison and co-ordination service. The service fee will be computed on a “cost plus 5%” basis. Estimated annual running costs for SS is in the region of $1.5m.

Advise A on all the income tax and GST implications of SS and this ASEAN distribution initiative and provide alternative suggestions where applicable.

Consider the time, place and value of supply!

Time: Earlier of invoice and payment date

Place: Goods are in Singapore at time of supply = within scope supplies!
If the goods are supplied to a place in Singapore = standard-rated.
If the goods are supplied to a place outside Singapore (exported) = zero-rated supply. This means that the GST is 0% but this is different from being out of scope because the business can still claim input tax.

Place: Goods are outside Singapore at time of supply = out-of-scope supplies!. Note: goods!!
Place (Services): Supplier "belongs" in Singapore at the time of supply = within scope supplies!.
If the supplier "belongs" outside Singapore at the time of supply = out-of-scope supplies! but is subject to reverse charge & OVR. Note: services!!

"Belonging": Supplier belongs in Singapore if Singapore is the only place of business. If the supplier has multiple places of business, the place of business that is most directly connected to the supply is the place of belonging.

Value of supply: Amount on which GST is calculated.
Open market value: Amount payable by a person who has no relationship with the supplier.
GST collected by the C&E Department at the point of importation is based on the value of import

B2B

Recall that if the supplier "belongs" outside Singapore at the time of supply, the supply is out-of-scope but subject to reverse charge.

What is reverse charge?

Let's say A is a Singaporean company and B is a foreign company. B provides services from overseas to A that cost $100k.
A would "charge" itself GST of $9k (output GST, not input) as though it is making the supply to itself. As this is a business purchase, A can claim the $9k as its input GST (remember input tax is from purchasing, output tax is from charging it to customers).
Reverse charge doesn't apply to fully taxable persons. It only applies to partially exempt and exempt traders.

Recall that if the supplier "belongs" outside Singapore at the time of supply, the supply is out-of-scope but subject to OVR.

What is Overseas Vendor Registration (OVR)

Overseas vendors must register for GST if:
  • Global revenue >$1mil per annum
  • Singapore revenue >$100k per annum
They charge GST if the Singaporean customer is not GST-registered. Otherwise, they won't.

Major Exporter Scheme (MES)

No GST on importation!! Given that they fulfil these conditions:
  • GST registered
  • Import goods in usual course of business
  • Export >50% or >$10mil per annum
  • Good accounting system
  • Good track record of income tax, GST, custom and excise, PT

Zero GST Warehouse Scheme

GST suspended on importation!! Given that the operators fulfil these conditions:
  • Registered with GST and C&E
  • Good track record with IRAS and C&E
  • Have designatied premises for zero GST area in storage-based facility!!
  • Have good inventory control
  • Comply with record-keeping requirements
    • Type I
      • Storage of ONLY imported goods
    • Type II
      • Storage of imported goods and local goods
    • Type III
      • Storage of imported goods, local goods
      • Multiple storage facilities
      • Goods can move from one storage facility to another

Approved Third Party Logistics (3PL)

No GST on importation!! Given that they fulfil these conditions:
  • Singapore registered company
  • GST registered
  • Logistics service provider
  • Use computerised WMS
  • Good internal control
  • Assume full responsibility of GST
  • Good track record for income tax, GST, customer and excise, PT

Approved Contract Manufacturer and Trader Scheme (ACMT)

This applies to contract manufacturers and logistics companies. They have to assume full GST responsibility for sales to local customers.
No GST on importation!!
No GST on delivery of goods to another ACMT!!
No GST on value-added services provided.
ACMT is applicable to the semiconductor industry, printing industry, and pharmaceutical and biomedical industry.
