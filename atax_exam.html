Advanced Taxation Revision

Question

A is a publisher of educational books. It is planning to use Singapore as the ASEAN base for the distribution of books published in the UK.

Under this plan, A will arrange to have books imported into Singapore for distribution to distributors in the various ASEAN countries. The contracts for the sale of books will be concluded directly by A with the various ASEAN country distributors and these distributors will be invoiced directly by A.

The revenue from these ASEAN distributors will be booked as A’s revenue and it is estimated that annual sales to the ASEAN region will be in the region of S$8m to S$10m. All the logistics will be undertaken by an unrelated logistics company operating in Singapore.

For the coordination of its ASEAN distribution activity, A will incorporate a wholly owned subsidiary in Singapore (SS). SS will be responsible for the liaison and co-ordination of the importation and delivery of books to the distributors in the various ASEAN countries and will receive a service fee from OUP for this liaison and co-ordination service. The service fee will be computed on a “cost plus 5%” basis. Estimated annual running costs for SS is in the region of $1.5m.

Advise A on all the income tax and GST implications of SS and this ASEAN distribution initiative and provide alternative suggestions where applicable.

Companies that have a turnover of more than $1mil must register for GST. The turnover includes:
  • Standard-rated supplies
  • Zero-rated supplies
but excludes:
  • Exempt supplies
  • Out-of-scope supplies
The determination of turnover can be based on retrospect. For example, the turnover in the last 12 months to 31 December. It can also be prospective, based on the expected turnover in the next 12 months.

EXEMPT supplies:
  • Financial services

OUT-OF-SCOPE supplies:
  • Payment of salaries and wages

Taxable supplies:

STANDARD-RATED supplies
  • Sale of food
  • Sale of machine

ZERO-RATED supplies
  • Export of goods
  • International services (Export of services)
Consider the time, place and value of supply!

Time: Earlier of invoice and payment date

Place: Goods are in Singapore at time of supply = within scope supplies!
If the goods are supplied to a place in Singapore, it will be standard-rated.
If the goods are supplied to a place outside Singapore (exported), it is a zero-rated supply. This means that the GST is 0% but this is different from being out of scope because the business can still claim input tax.
